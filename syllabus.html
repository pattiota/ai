Course Description

This course will explore the best consulting practices around project management of large-scale business software systems, such as ERP systems. It will examine the role of consultants in mitigating the risks involved in such implementations.

What You'll Learn

ERP systems are comprehensive applications that support and connect all aspects of an organization's business processes including Accounting, Human Resources, Supply-Chain Management, Marketing, Manufacturing, etc.

Course Focus

Students will study actual, present-day ERP implementations and hear from consultants who have been involved in this type of work. Topics will include:

  • The project life cycle
  • Project management
  • Implementation methodologies
  • Change management
  • Analytics
  • How businesses use Generative AI
  • How consultants impact the implementation process

Current Technology Trends

Current trends in technology will be explored, such as:

  • Distributed cloud providers
  • Generative AI
  • Personalization
  • Digital identity

Hands-On Experience

Students will have the opportunity to work in teams to explore a current project and produce a critical consultant ERP report.

Course Materials

Course materials will be available directly on D2L.

Modality

This course will be delivered using in-person modality.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will achieve the following learning outcomes:

  • Understand the role of consultants in the implementation process of large-scale business software systems
  • Practice active listening, reading, and recording of key information in a clear, concise, and organized way
  • Develop a practical understanding of the degree to which businesses and organizations benefit from and rely upon ERP systems to understand and manage their operations
  • Understand overall technology trends that are affecting the opportunities, strategies, and technologies that will drive new plans for enterprise management
  • Develop real-world experience solving a current business problem

Course Work

Class Participation

In-class discussions on the presentations and readings are significant aspects of the class. Individual participation will be evaluated in terms of voluntary, courteous contribution of ideas; thoughtful questions; and demonstration of critical thinking skills.

Class Summary Memos

Consultants deal with large volumes of information—client meetings, interviews, workshops, documents. Good note-taking ensures critical points aren't missed. A good summary helps everyone focus on what's essential—key issues, decisions, next steps—without getting lost in the weeds.

The Summary Memos help students develop their note-taking and summarizing skills.

Short Presentations

Throughout the semester, students will be asked to prepare short presentations/summaries about course-related topics. These presentations will be evaluated in terms of clarity of ideas, accuracy, organization, and thoughtful responses to follow-up questions.

Major Group Presentation

There are two main reasons for in-class presentations: sharing what you have learned with your classmates for their educational benefit and equipping you to make strong and effective presentations to others in the "real world" when you need to.

Presentation Requirements:

  • Groups will identify a topic about an emerging technology (instructor approved)
  • 30-45 minutes in length
  • Professional in-class presentation
  • Share information with the entire class

Presentations will be evaluated in terms of technical content, organization, quality of presentation materials (PowerPoint slides), coordination and clear delineation of responsibilities, and handouts.

Group Project

This course includes a group project regarding a forward-thinking technology project.

Project Requirements:

  • Written report: Minimum length of 12 pages
  • References: At least 3 references with appropriate citations in MLA or APA format
  • Due Date: December 5th, 2025

Projects will be evaluated in terms of document components (Executive Summary, Body (heft, depth, style), Syntax (grammar, voice consistency), References and Evidence Cited, and other criteria such as did authors get the point of the assignment and did they demonstrate an understanding of ERP systems.

White Paper (MIS 527 Students Only)

A "white paper" on an approved ERP topic of at least 2000 words with word count. A white paper is an authoritative report or guide that informs readers concisely about a complex issue and presents the issuing body's philosophy on the matter.

Requirements:

  • At least 3 non-AI references with appropriate citations in MLA or APA format
  • Due December 5th, 2025

Final Examination

Oral final examination will be conducted during the last week of classes. This exam format provides the opportunity to better assess the understanding of course material including information covered in class presentations, discussions, and group projects.

AI Policy

🤖 Responsible AI Usage

Using AI responsibly means that you can use AI tools, like ChatGPT, to help generate ideas or clarify concepts. AI is a support tool, not a substitute for your own work.

Guidelines:

  • AI can be used to help generate ideas or clarify concepts
  • Submissions that appear to be copied from AI with little modification or without proper citation may result in reduced or no credit
  • AI is a tool, not a teacher
  • You still need to actively think, experiment, and reflect to truly learn

Remember: It's super powerful, but you still need to actively think, experiment, and reflect to truly learn. If you have any questions/doubts about this policy, please ask.

University Policies

Academic Integrity

Students assume full responsibility for the content and integrity of the academic work they submit. The guiding principle of academic integrity shall be that all submitted work including assignments, examinations, reports, projects, etc. must be that student's own work alone, unless clearly following the rules for allowable group work.

Students shall be guilty of violating the Code and be subject to proceedings under it if they cheat, fabricate, plagiarize, and represent others work as their own. You are responsible for reading and understanding the College's and University's policy at https://academicaffairs.arizona.edu/syllabus-policies.

Potential Penalties for Academic Dishonesty:

  • A failing grade for the entire assessment item without the option of make-up or replacement
  • A letter grade drop in the final class grade
  • A failing grade for the entire course

The course instructor makes the final penalty determination. In case of any accusations of academic dishonesty, the student case may be forwarded to the Office of the Dean of Students.
